International student migration around the world - origins and destinations

by Ta Quang Tung, Nguyen Quang Huy

Thanks to greater economic prosperity and the convenience of international travel, people have become more mobile than before. No longer are people tied down to their country of origin - they can now travel the world to look for a better life in other countries. Among these migrants, many leave their homes in search of better educational opportunities. Between 2017 and 2021, an estimated 30 million people migrated to study overseas. These international students play an important cultural and socio-economic role in the places they arrive at. This visualization project aims to give readers an overview of international student migration by looking at where these students came from and where they headed.

Origins of international students

China and India were the leading origin countries. Over the period, Vietnam increased in ranking, going from 6 to 3. Uzbekistan and the Syrian Arab Republic saw the biggest jumps in rankings out of all. In addition to developing Asian and African countries, some wealthier Western countries like Germany, the USA, and France were also present in the ranking. Asian students generally preferred Western destinations (see China, India, South Korea), while European students preferred other European countries (see Germany, France, Italy).

Destinations of international students

European and American countries occupied the majority of the destination ranking. The USA, the UK, and Australia were the top 3 destinations. European countries like Germany, Russia, and France followed. Wealthier Asian nations like South Korea and Japan also appeared in the list, and their rankings increased over the period. That said, the majority of students who came to these Asian countries were from Asia.

Acknowledgements

The data for this visualization project has been collected from the data portal of UNESCO Institute for Statistics (UIS) The data sets used are: Inbound internationally mobile students by country of origin, and Outbound internationally mobile students by host region. This data set is not comprehensive and data for certain years or countries is not available. However, for the sake of integrity, we have avoided replacing missing data with data from other sources.
